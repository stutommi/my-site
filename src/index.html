Music

Short Bio

I grew up most of my childhood in Kainuu. I started getting interested about guitar playing seriously when I was around 13 years old. I've played and studied music from year to year ever since. I finished my latest studies when I graduated from Metropolia (UAS) in 2018.

Currently I'm gigging and teaching mainly in and around Helsinki area.

What's Second Wind Quintet?

Second Wind Quintet is a jazz/folk-oriented group which performs original instrumental music every now and then around Finland.

SWQ includes:
Tommi Tampio Guitar
Kasper Haikonen Horns
Aapo Wikstén Piano
Juuso Rinta Bass
Ilkka Oksanen Drums

We have a debut album in the making. Stay tuned!

While waiting, you can listen to some of our earlier recordings from Youtube or Soundcloud!

Web Developing

Short History

(let experience = 0; experience < lifetime; experience++)

It started at my friends place on fall 2018. We had no idea what coding was about and I started googling what does it actually mean. My friend found codecademy and we played with it for a while.

After that I decided that I would try to learn some basics of it to see if I could build anything useful out of it. I started with javascript course on codecademy. I found out I enjoyed the logic and the challenge it holds. After completing it I was hungry for more so I started codecademys web development path which is a little bigger slice to swallow (basics in HTML, CSS, JS, Jquery, MySQL, Github, React, Node, Express).

Codecademys lessons are managed into nice bite-sized pieces and they are rewarding and fun to do. But I was still longing to make something useful with the skills I learned. One funny and challenging (and ongoing) project is automated plant-watering system I'm making with my brother. There's some info about it in projects section.

After finishing codecademys web development path I've been learning mainly with different tutorials, own projects, Stack Overflow and Google. When I'll see some awesome way to present something one the web I'll strive to recreate it myself. My motivation to develop my skills have only grown since the start!
